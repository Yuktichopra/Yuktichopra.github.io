Research

My research explores the intersection of computational neuroscience, cognitive psychology, and brain imaging to understand how we think, remember, and make decisions.

Key Research Areas

Working Memory Dynamics(Current)

Investigating how the brain temporarily stores and manipulates information using EEG and computational modeling.

  • • Neural oscillations in working memory
  • • Capacity limitations and individual differences
  • • Computational models of memory maintenance

Attention Networks(Current)

Studying how attention systems coordinate to filter information and guide behavior.

  • • EEG markers of attentional control
  • • Interaction between attention and memory
  • • Attention training interventions

Cognitive Aging(Ongoing)

Examining how cognitive functions change across the lifespan and identifying protective factors.

  • • Memory decline and preservation
  • • Neuroplasticity in aging
  • • Lifestyle interventions for cognitive health
